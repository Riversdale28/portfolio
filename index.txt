        <h1 class="page-title">Significant Cyber Incidents</h1>
<p>This timeline records significant cyber incidents since 2006. We focus on cyber attacks on government agencies, defense and high tech companies, or economic crimes with losses of more than a million dollars.</p>

<center>
<hr /><a href="https://csis-website-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/s3fs-public/220203_Significant_Cyber_Incidents.pdf?6nUHMBGT7zrGtFIeHU4gGdjD7dXFObfO"><strong>Download the Full Incidents List</strong></a></center>

<hr />
<h3><strong>Below is a summary of incidents from over the last year. For the full list, click the download link above.</strong></h3>

<p><strong>January 2022. </strong> A Belarusian hacktivist group accessed the networks of state-owned Belarusian Railway. The group encrypted the majority of the Railway’s servers and destroyed data held on a backup server, possibly to complicate Russian troop movements throughout the country.</p>

<p><strong>January 2022. </strong> A Chinese hacking group breached several German pharma and tech firms. According to the German government, the hack into the networks of service providers and companies was primarily an attempt to steal intellectual property.</p>

<p><strong>January 2022. </strong> Hackers shut down internet traffic to and from North Korea twice in two weeks from what researchers say was likely a series of DDoS attacks. The second attack came just after North Korea’s 5<sup>th</sup> missile test of the month.</p>

<p><strong>January 2022. </strong> Hackers breached the Canadian Foreign Ministry, hampering some of the Ministry’s internet-connected services. The hack came a day after the government issued a warning to bolster network security in anticipation of Russia-based cyberattacks on critical infrastructure.</p>

<p><strong>January 2022. </strong> A series of DDoS attacks targeted a high-stakes Minecraft tournament and ended up impacting Andorra Telecom, the country's only internet service provider. The attack disrupted 4G and internet services for customers.</p>

<p><strong>January 2022. </strong> The Informatic Directorate of the Greek Parliament identified an attempt to hack into 60 parliamentary email accounts. In response, authorities temporarily shut down the mailing system in the legislature.</p>

<p><strong>January 2022. </strong> An Australian spokesman accused WeChat of taking down Prime Minister Scott Morrison’s account and redirecting users to a website that provides information for Chinese expatriates. The Government claims that they first encountered problems posting to the Prime Minister’s account in mid-2021.</p>

<p><strong>January 2022. </strong> Hackers breached systems belonging to the International Committee of the Red Cross, gaining access to data on more than 500,000 people and disrupting their services around the world.</p>

<p><strong>January 2022. </strong> A cyberattack targeted the Ukrainian government, hitting 90 websites and deploying malicious software masquerading as ransomware to damage dozens of computers in government agencies.</p>

<p><strong>January 2022. </strong> Hackers attacked several Israeli media outlets, including Maariv and the Jerusalem Post, posting threatening messages on their websites. One message stated "we are close to you where you do not think about it" in English and Hebrew.</p>

<p><strong>January 2022. </strong> A DRPK-affiliated group targeted multiple Russian diplomats with malware. The diplomats received an email disguised as a New Year greetings screensaver but which, after being opened, installed a remote access trojan.</p>

<p><strong>December 2021</strong> . A cyberattack on the Belgium Ministry of Defence forced part of its computer network, including the ministry’s mail system, to shut down for several days. Hackers exploited the Log4j vulnerability to compromise the network.</p>

<p><strong>December 2021.</strong> Hackers targeted multiple Southeast Asian governments over the past 9 months using custom malware linked to Chinese state-sponsored groups. Many of the nations targeted are currently engaged in disputes with China over territorial claims in the South China Sea.</p>

<p><strong>December 2021</strong> <strong>.</strong> A breach of Prime Minster Modi’s Twitter allowed hackers to Tweet from the account that India officially adopted bitcoin as legal tender. The Tweet also included a scam link promising a bitcoin giveaway.</p>

<p><strong>December 2021.</strong> A Bloomberg investigation publicly linked an intrusion into Australia’s telecommunications systems in 2012 to malicious code embedded in a software update from Huawei.</p>

<p><strong>December 2021. </strong> Cybersecurity firms found government-linked hackers from China, Iran, and North Korea attempting to use the Log4j vulnerability to gain access to computer networks. Following the announcement of Log4j, researchers already found over 600,000 attempts to exploit the vulnerability.</p>

&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><span data-ccp-parastyle="Body Text"><strong>May 2021.</strong></span></span><span data-contrast="auto"><span data-ccp-parastyle="Body Text">&nbsp;<span data-ccp-parastyle="Body Text">LineStar</span><span data-ccp-parastyle="Body Text">&nbsp;Integrity Services, a pipeline-focused business, was hit by a ransomware attack the same time as the Colonial Pipeline, with 70 gigabytes of its internal files being stolen.</span></span></span>&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><span data-ccp-parastyle="Body Text"><strong>May 2021.</strong></span></span><span data-contrast="auto"><span data-ccp-parastyle="Body Text">&nbsp;A North Korean cyberattack on South Korea's state-run Korea Atomic Energy Research Institute (KAERI) occurred through a vulnerability in a vendor’s VPN.</span>&nbsp;</span>&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>May 2021.&nbsp;</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">The world’s largest meat processing company, Brazilian-based JBS, was the victim of a ransomware attack. The attack shut down facilities in the United States, Canada and Australia. The attack was attributed to the Russian speaking cybercrime group,&nbsp;REvil</span>.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>May 2021.</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">&nbsp;On May 24th, hackers gained access to Fujitsu’s systems and stole files belonging to multiple Japanese government entities. So far four government agencies have been impacted.&nbsp;</span>&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>May 2021.</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">&nbsp;Cybersecurity researchers identified a North Korean hacking group to be responsible for</span>&nbsp;<span data-contrast="none">a cyber espionage campaign, targeting high profile South Korean government officials, utilizing a phishing methodology. The group’s targets were based in South Korea and included: the Korea Internet and Security Agency (KISA), ROK Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Ambassador of the Embassy of Sri Lanka to the State (in ROK), International Atomic Energy Agency Nuclear Security Officer, Deputy Consul General at Korean Consulate General in Hong Kong, Seoul National University, and Daishin Securities.</span>&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>May 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. On May 14, Ireland’s national health service, the Health Service Executive (HSE), was the victim of a ransomware attack. Upon discovering the attack, government authorities shut down the HSE system. The attackers utilized the Conti ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS), which is reported to be operated by a Russia-based cybercrime group.</span>&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>May 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. The FBI and the Australian Cyber Security Centre warned of an ongoing&nbsp;Avaddon</span>&nbsp;ransomware campaign targeting multiple sectors in various countries. The reported targeted countries are Australia, Belgium, Brazil, Canada, China, Costa Rica, Czech Republic, France, Germany, India, Indonesia, Italy, Jordan, Peru, Poland, Portugal, Spain, UAE, UK, US. The targeted industries include: academia, airlines, construction, energy, equipment, financial, freight, government, health, it, law enforcement, manufacturing, marketing, retail, pharmaceutical.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>May 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. On May 6, the Colonial Pipeline, the largest fuel pipeline in the United States, was the target of a ransomware attack. The energy company shut down the pipeline and later paid a $5 million ransom. The attack is attributed to&nbsp;DarkSide</span>, a Russian speaking hacking group.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>May 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. On May 4th and 5th, the Norwegian energy technology company&nbsp;Volue</span>&nbsp;was the victim of a ransomware attack. The attack resulted in the shutdown of water and water treatment facilities in 200 municipalities, affecting approximately 85% of the Norwegian population.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>May 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. A large DDoS attack disabled the ISP used by Belgium’s government,&nbsp;impacting more than 200 organizations&nbsp;</span>causing the cancellation of&nbsp;multiple Parliamentary meetings&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>May 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. A Chinese hacking group compromised a Russian defense contractor involved in designing nuclear submarines for the Russian navy.</span>&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>April 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">.&nbsp;A hacking group&nbsp;</span>compromised&nbsp;the social media accounts of Polish officials and used them to&nbsp;disseminate&nbsp;narratives critical of NATO. German authorities have reported that the same group has also attempted to compromise members of the Bundestag and state parliament.&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>April 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. Hackers linked to the Chinese military conducted an espionage campaign targeting military and government organizations in Southeast Asia beginning in 2019</span>&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>April 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. Malware triggered an outage for airline reservation systems that caused the networks of 20 low-cost airlines around the world to crash.</span>&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>April 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. Russian hackers targeted Ukrainian government officials with&nbsp;spearphishing</span>&nbsp;attempts&nbsp;as tensions between the two nations rose during early 2021.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>April 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">.&nbsp;Hackers linked to Palestinian intelligence conducted a cyber espionage campaign&nbsp;</span>compromising approximately 800&nbsp;Palestinian reporters, activists, and dissidents both in Palestine and more broadly across the Middle East.&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>April 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">.&nbsp;Two state-backed hacking groups—one of which works on behalf of the Chinese government—exploited vulnerabilities in a VPN service to target&nbsp;</span>organizations across the U.S. and Europe with a particular focus on U.S. defense contractors.&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>April 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. MI5 warned that over 10,000 UK professional shave been targeted by hostile states over the past five years as part of&nbsp;spearphishing</span>&nbsp;and social engineering campaigns on LinkedIn.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>April 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">.&nbsp;Swedish officials disclosed that t</span>he Swedish Sports&nbsp;Confederation was hacked by Russian military intelligence in late 2017 and early 2018 in response to accusations of Russian government-sponsored doping of Russian athletes.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>April 2021.</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">&nbsp;New York City's Metropolitan Transportation Authority (MTA) was hacked by Chinese-backed actors but were unable to gain access to user data or information systems.&nbsp;&nbsp;</span>&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>April 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. French security researchers found that the number of attacks hitting critical French businesses increased fourfold in 2020 during the COVID-19 pandemic.</span>&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>April 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. The European Commission announced that the EC and multiple other EU organizations were hit by a major cyberattack&nbsp;by unknow</span>n hackers.&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>April 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. Chinese hackers launched a months-long cyber espionage campaign&nbsp;during the second half of 2020&nbsp;</span>targeting government agencies in Vietnam&nbsp;with the intent of gathering&nbsp;political intelligence&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>March 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">.&nbsp;The North Korean hacking group responsible for a set of attacks on&nbsp;</span>cybersecurity researchers in January 2021 launched a new campaign&nbsp;targeting infosec professionals&nbsp;using fake social media profiles and a fake website for a non-existent&nbsp;security&nbsp;service&nbsp;company&nbsp;target.&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>March 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. Suspected Iranian hackers targeted medical researchers in Israel and the U.S.&nbsp;in an attempt to steal the credentials of geneticists, neurologists, and oncologists in the two countries</span>.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>March 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. Suspected Russian hackers stole thousands of emails after breaching the email server of the U.S. State Department</span>.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>March 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. Suspected state hackers targeted the Australian media company Nine Entertainment with a ransomware variant, disrupting live broadcasts and print production systems.&nbsp;</span>&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>March 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">.&nbsp;Suspected Russian hackers&nbsp;</span>attempted to gain access to the personal email accounts of German parliamentarians in the run-up to Germany’s national elections.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>March 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. U.S. Cyber Command confirmed that it was assisting Columbia in responding to election interference and influence operations.</span>&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>March 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. The head of U.S. Cyber Command testified that&nbsp;the organization had conducted more than two dozen operations to confront foreign threats ahead of the 2020 U.S. elections, including eleven forward hunt operations in nine different countries.&nbsp;</span>&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>March 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. A group of Chinese hackers used Facebook&nbsp;to send malicious links to&nbsp;</span>Uyghur&nbsp;activists, journalists, and dissidents&nbsp;located&nbsp;abroad.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>March 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. The Indian Computer Emergency Response Team found evidence of Chinese&nbsp;hackers conducting a cyber espionage campaign against the Indian transportation sector</span>.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>March 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. Polish security services announced that&nbsp;suspected&nbsp;</span>Russian hackers&nbsp;briefly&nbsp;took over the websites of Poland’s National Atomic Energy Agency and Health Ministry to spread false alerts of a nonexistent radioactive threat.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>March 2021.&nbsp;</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">Both&nbsp;Russian and Chinese intelligence services targeted the European Medicines Agency in 2020</span>&nbsp;in unrelated campaigns, stealing documents relating to COVID-19 vaccines and medicines.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>March 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">.&nbsp;Ukraine’s</span>&nbsp;State Security Service announced it had prevented a large-scale attack by Russian FSB hackers attempting to gain access to classified government data.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>March 2021.</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">&nbsp;Lithuania’s State Security Department declared that Russian hackers had targeted top Lithuanian officials in 2020 and used the country’s IT infrastructure to carry out attacks against organizations involved in developing a C</span>OVID-19 vaccine.&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>March 2021.&nbsp;</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">Suspected Iranian hackers&nbsp;targeted government agencies, academia, and the tourism industry in Azerbaijan, Bahrain, Israel, Saudi Arabia, and the UAE as part of a cyber espionage campaign.</span>&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="none"><strong>March 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="none">. Chinese government hackers targeted Microsoft’s enterprise email software to steal data from&nbsp;over 30,000</span>&nbsp;organizations&nbsp;around the world,&nbsp;including government agencies, legislative bodies,&nbsp;law firms, defense contractors, infectious disease researchers, and policy think tanks.&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><strong>March 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="auto">. Suspected Chinese hackers&nbsp;</span>targeted electricity grid operators in India in an apparent attempt to lay the groundwork for&nbsp;possible&nbsp;future attacks.&nbsp;&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><strong>February 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="auto">.&nbsp;</span>A Portuguese-speaking&nbsp;cyber criminal&nbsp;group&nbsp;accessed computer systems at&nbsp;a division of Oxford University researching COVID-19&nbsp;vaccines,&nbsp;and are suspected to be selling the data&nbsp;they collected&nbsp;to nation states.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><strong>February 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="auto">.&nbsp;</span>North Korean hackers&nbsp;targeted defense firms in more than a dozen countries in an espionage campaign starting in early 2020.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><strong>February 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span>&nbsp;<span data-contrast="auto">Hackers associated with the Chinese military conducted a surveillance campaign against Tibetans both in China and abroad</span>.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><strong>February 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="auto">. Russian hackers compromised a Ukrainian government file-sharing system and attempted to disseminate malicious documents&nbsp;</span>that would install malware on computers that downloaded the planted files.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><strong>February 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="auto">. Hackers linked to the Vietnamese government conducted a nearly thre</span>e-year&nbsp;cyber espionage campaign against human rights advocates in the&nbsp;country by using spyware to infiltrate individuals’ systems, spy on their activity, and exfiltrate data.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><strong>February 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="auto">.&nbsp;</span>Ukrainian officials reported that a&nbsp;multi-day distributed denial-of-service attack&nbsp;against the website of the Security Service of Ukraine&nbsp;was part of Russia’s hybrid warfare&nbsp;operations in the country.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><strong>February 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="auto">. The US Department of Justice indicted three North Korean hackers for conspiring to steal and extort more than $1.3 billion in cash and cryptocurrencies</span>.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><strong>February 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="auto">. Iranian hackers&nbsp;</span>took control of a server in Amsterdam and used it as a&nbsp;command and control&nbsp;center for attacks against political opponents in the Netherlands, Germany,&nbsp;Sweden, and India.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><strong>February 2021.&nbsp;</strong></span><span data-contrast="auto">North Korean hackers attempted to break into the computer systems of pharmaceutical company&nbsp;</span>Pfizer&nbsp;to gain information about vaccines and treatments for the&nbsp;COVID-19.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><strong>February 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="auto">. Suspected Iranian hackers targeted government agencies in the UAE</span>&nbsp;as part of a cyber espionage campaign related to the normalizations of relations with Israel.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><strong>February 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="auto">. The French national cybersecurity agency announced that a four-year campaign against French IT providers was the work of a Russian hacking group</span>.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><strong>February 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="auto">. Suspected Indian hackers targeted over 150 individuals in Pakistan, Kazakhstan, and India using mobile malware, including those wi</span>th links to the Pakistan Atomic Energy Commission, the Pakistan Air Force, and election officials in Kashmir.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><strong>February 2021</strong></span><span data-contrast="auto">.&nbsp;</span>Ten members of a&nbsp;cybercriminal gang were&nbsp;arrested after a campaign where they tricked telecom companies into assigning celebrities’ phone numbers to new devices, stealing more than $100 million worth of cryptocurrencies.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><strong>February 2021.&nbsp;</strong></span><span data-contrast="auto">Unknown hackers attempted to raise levels of sodium hydroxide in the water supply of&nbsp;</span>Oldsmar, Florida&nbsp;by a factor of 100 by exploiting a remote access system.&nbsp;<br />
&nbsp;<br />
<span data-contrast="auto"><strong>February 2021.&nbsp;</strong></span><span data-contrast="auto">Two Iranian hacking groups conducted espionage campaigns against Iranian dissidents in&nbsp;</span>sixteen countries in the Middle East, Europe, South Asia, and North America.</p>
